✨ Strategic Social System

×
1. Reduce Decisions, Not Increase Them
Predefine categories and rotation rules. Let a system (not mood) decide who/what next to save mental energy.
2. Use 3 Simple Social Buckets
Maintain Light touch: Texting, checking in (High frequency, low effort).
Deepen Shared experiences: Coffee, dinner, walks (Growth).
Support Intentional effort: Helping, favors (High impact).
3. Categorize by “Closeness Tier”
Tier 1 (Inner, 3-8): High priority, frequent contact.
Tier 2 (Active, 8-20): Friends to grow. Medium frequency.
Tier 3 (Outer, Unlimited): Acquaintances. Low frequency.
4. Weekly “Social Rotation”
Aim for 3-4 actions/week: 2× Maintain, 1× Deepen, 1× Support (optional).
5. Use a “Rolling Queue”
Move people to the bottom after contact; always pick from the top. Ensures automatic fairness.
6. Frequency Rules per Tier
Tier 1: 1-2 weeks | Tier 2: 3-6 weeks | Tier 3: 2-4 months. Move up if they feel "due".
7. Make It Frictionless
Set recurring reminders: 2×/week for "Social Maintenance" (10m), 1×/week for "Plan Something" (15m).
8. “Default Action” Rule
Tired? Maintain. Normal? Deepen. Motivated? Support. No overthinking.
9. Prevent Overwhelm
Max 1 planned event/week. Max 3 outreach messages/session. Skip guilt.
10. Light Tracking
Only track last contact and next idea (e.g., "walk"). Avoid detailed logs.
